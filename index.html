About me

At the University of UNC-Chapel Hill, my academic background in Statistics and Analytics & Computer Science drives my commitment to expanding access to learning resources. As Technical Chair of AI@UNC, I've spearheaded initiatives like NeuroSpark, promoting novel approaches to awake craniotomy research and AI research. Presenting my research internationally at Oxford University allowed me to share insights from my research on undergraduate mental health and to advocate for novel approaches to management of mental health resources. At the BRANE Lab, my research has focused on the machine learning methods behind neurobiological and behavioral determinants of substance use treatment response. Through leadership roles and research, I strive to build a healthier, more well-informed community.
